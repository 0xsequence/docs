# MOCKDATA + ja-translated How to Deploy in-game Currency in Sequence Builder

## ja-translated Introduction
MOCKDATA + ja-translated In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of creating your own in-game currency through deploying and minting from an ERC20 contract.

ja-translated Currency tokens add new and interesting dimensions to in-game economies, transactions, and player interactions to unlock and distribute value.

#### MOCKDATA + ja-translated What is an ERC20 token?
ja-translated An ERC20 token is a digital coin - what makes it special is that it follows a [set of rules called ERC20](https://ethereum.org/en/developers/docs/standards/tokens/erc-20/), making it easy to use in different places on the internet. These tokens often represent value or be used for specific purposes. Unlike the other common token - NFT's, these tokens are fungible.

#### MOCKDATA + ja-translated Fungibility vs Non-Fungibility
ja-translated This is the key distinction to understand.

MOCKDATA + ja-translated Fungible tokens (like ERC20 tokens) are interchangeable, and each unit is the same as every other unit, just like dollars or euros. They can easily be split or combined into different quantities.

ja-translated Non-fungible tokens (like ERC721 tokens) represent unique items with individual properties, such as digital art or collectibles. Each token is distinct and not directly exchangeable with another on a like-for-like basis.

## ja-translated Step 1: Navigate to contracts
MOCKDATA + ja-translated Start by selecting your `project` in the top left and corner for what you want to create the currency for, and head to the `contracts` section and select `+ Deploy new contract`

ja-translated ![select project and new contract](/img/collectibles/select_project_new_contract.png)

## MOCKDATA + ja-translated Step 2: Select Currency Token
ja-translated Click `View more contracts`

MOCKDATA + ja-translated ![view more contracts](/img/collectibles/view_more_contracts.png)

ja-translated Select the Currency Token contract and click `deploy` to deploy your Sequence audited contract

MOCKDATA + ja-translated ![select currency and deploy button](/img/collectibles/select_currency_deploy.png)

## MOCKDATA + ja-translated Step 3: Specify contract details
ja-translated Complete the contract details by specifying a `name` and `symbol` for your contract.

MOCKDATA + ja-translated ![select currency and deploy button](/img/collectibles/currency_contract_details.png)

<Callout type="danger">
  ja-translated Note:

  While you can change the `name` and `symbol` later in the builder interface where it will update across the sequence stack, popular explorers do not reindex the information, so what you put first remains
</Callout>

## MOCKDATA + ja-translated Step 4: Deploy your contract
ja-translated Deploy your contract from the popup window at [http://sequence.app/sign-transaction](http://sequence.app/sign-transaction), and sign the message by selecting `confirm`

<img src="/img/collectibles/sign_deploy_transaction.png" width="200" />

<Callout type="info">
  ja-translated All testnets will be free to transact on
</Callout>

## MOCKDATA + ja-translated Step 5: Add a Minter Role to the contract
ja-translated First start by selecting your contract you just deployed in the `contracts` section

ja-translated ![select contract](/img/collectibles/select_contract.png)

ja-translated Next, head to the `Write Contract` section

MOCKDATA + ja-translated ![write contract](/img/collectibles/select_write_contract.png)

ja-translated In the `grantRole` section of the write contract tab navigation, complete with the following details:

MOCKDATA + ja-translated `bytes32 role`: `0x9f2df0fed2c77648de5860a4cc508cd0818c85b8b8a1ab4ceeef8d981c8956a6`
`address account`: `<Wallet Address>`

MOCKDATA + ja-translated Where the wallet address is just copied from the top right hand corner to be able to send tokens to your wallet

ja-translated ![copy address](/img/collectibles/copy_address.png)

MOCKDATA + ja-translated ![grant role](/img/collectibles/grant_role.png)

ja-translated Complete by selecting `write` and signing the transaction in the popup window, like before

MOCKDATA + ja-translated With a confirmation message showing `Response Success`

<Callout type="info">
  ja-translated The role string inputted is the result of `solidityPackedKeccak256("MINTER_ROLE")` in
  solidity or `ethers.solidityPackedKeccak256(ethers.toUtf8Bytes("MINTER_ROLE"))`
  in javascript
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
  ja-translated It's a typical pattern to use offchain compute like a cloudflare worker with a relayer wallet sending transactions, which would be inputted in the account field, more on how to accomplish this [here](/guides/mint-collectibles-serverless#mint-collectibles-using-a-gasless-serverless-transactions-api)
</Callout>

## ja-translated Step 6: Mint tokens to your wallet address
MOCKDATA + ja-translated Navigate to the `mint` card in the `Write Contract` section and input your wallet address that you would like to receive tokens to and amount of tokens in 18 decimals. So for 100 tokens you would input: `100000000000000000000`

ja-translated ![mint tokens](/img/collectibles/mint_currency.png)

## MOCKDATA + ja-translated Step 7: Confirm your minted currency
